# Customer Feedback Process A-to-B Plan

## A: Current Position

- Support manually pastes customer feedback into a shared spreadsheet.
- Product managers review the collected feedback inconsistently.
- Support cannot see what happened to each submitted item.
- The shared spreadsheet is the current operational record for customer feedback.

## B: Desired Position

The ad-hoc spreadsheet is replaced by a dependable customer-feedback process that carries every item from a single intake route through legal redaction and Thursday triage by the customer research team to a final disposition visible to support.

## Path from A to B

1. **Define the feedback lifecycle and ownership.** Establish the stages, responsibilities, and disposition model needed to move an item from intake through legal redaction and customer research triage to a visible outcome.
2. **Establish the single intake route.** Create one operational route for support to submit customer feedback into a consistent feedback record that can proceed to legal redaction.
3. **Establish legal redaction before triage.** Ensure every submitted item completes the required legal redaction before it can enter the customer research triage queue.
4. **Establish Thursday customer research triage.** Give the customer research team ownership of a queue reviewed every Thursday, with triage resulting in a final disposition for each item.
5. **Make dispositions visible to support.** Close the feedback loop by ensuring support can find the final disposition of every submitted item.
6. **Transition away from the shared spreadsheet.** Move the operating process to the new route and retire the spreadsheet as the active feedback tracker once the replacement process covers intake, redaction, triage, and disposition visibility.

## C: Observable Outcomes

- Customer feedback enters the operating process through one intake route.
- Every item completes legal redaction before entering the triage queue.
- The customer research team reviews the triage queue every Thursday.
- Every submitted item receives a final disposition.
- Support can see the final disposition of every item it submits.
- The shared spreadsheet is no longer used as the active customer-feedback process.
