# Repeatable Service Launch A-to-B Plan

## A: Current Position

- Service launches are performed through manual steps that each team improvises.
- The Operations Rollout Checklist is the authoritative launch-process input. It requires confirmation of support coverage, publication of escalation ownership, and verification of operational monitoring.

## B: Desired Position

- Service launches follow one repeatable process that replaces team-specific improvisation.

## Path from A to B

1. **Establish the launch-process contract.** Define the process boundary, participating roles, required inputs, and decision responsibilities so every team begins from the same expectations.
2. **Standardize operational-readiness preparation.** Use the Operations Rollout Checklist to produce completed evidence covering support coverage, escalation ownership, and operational monitoring.
3. **Review operational-readiness evidence.** Require the support lead to review the completed evidence and verify operational readiness before the launch decision is considered.
4. **Make the reviewed launch decision.** Reach and record the launch decision after the support lead's operational-readiness review has been completed.
5. **Adopt and validate the repeatable process.** Transition teams from improvised launches to the shared sequence and confirm that completed launches produce the required evidence and outcomes.

## C: Completion Evidence

- Teams can carry out service launches through the same repeatable process instead of inventing steps for each launch.
- Each launch produces completed operational-readiness evidence that addresses the Operations Rollout Checklist requirements.
- Each launch records the support lead's verification of the completed operational-readiness evidence before the launch decision.
- Each launch produces a reviewed launch decision before proceeding.
