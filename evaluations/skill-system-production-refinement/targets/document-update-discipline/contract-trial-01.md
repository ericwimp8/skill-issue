# Document Update Discipline: Target Outcome Interpretation

## Goal

Prevent proximal document patching by making semantic ownership, rather than the location where a gap was noticed, determine where and how an update is integrated.

## Intended Use

Use whenever an agent plans or performs an update to any document. The workflow becomes mandatory once loaded and must be completed before editing.

## Expected Behavior

The agent must read enough of the whole document to identify its purpose, structure, existing treatment of the requested meaning, and related authoritative wording; separate the observation point from the semantic home and other manifestations; state the required post-update meaning and preserved unrelated meanings; choose the necessary operation; then make the smallest complete update at the semantic owner. Afterward, it must reconcile related sections, examples, and cross-references, remove conflicts or duplicate ownership, preserve tone and unrelated meaning, and reframe instead of editing when ownership remains unclear.

## Observable Expected Result

For an editing task, the resulting document expresses the requested current truth coherently at its owning location, with related manifestations reconciled, contradictions and unintended duplication removed, unrelated meaning intact, and no visible sign of a proximity-driven patch. For a planning task, the observable result is an ownership-backed update decision that identifies the semantic home, related manifestations, required operation, and preservation boundaries before any edit.

## Preserved Boundaries

- The user-requested meaning must be preserved without automatically adopting suggested wording or an insertion point.
- A local edit remains valid when the local location genuinely owns the idea.
- Whole-document reading may be limited only when the user limits scope and accepts the risk.
- The update must remain the smallest complete change; the contract does not authorize unrelated rewriting.
- Historical transition wording is appropriate only when the document itself is historical.
- Evaluation convenience must not redefine, widen, or narrow this contract.

## Evidence

- The target states that observation begins investigation while semantic ownership decides the update location.
- Its required decision demands explicit post-update meaning, document purpose and structure, location mapping, operation choice, and preservation of unrelated meanings.
- Its workflow requires whole-document understanding, semantic-owner establishment, complete reconciliation, and final verification.
- Its stop conditions require remapping when convenience, unclear ownership, incomplete reconciliation, weakened unrelated meaning, or user pushback invalidates the framing.

## Ambiguity

The description includes both planning and editing, while most workflow completion language concerns an actual edit. The contract clearly requires the ownership analysis before either activity; for planning-only use, the most supportable observable result is the completed update decision and semantic map rather than a modified document.
