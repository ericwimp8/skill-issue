# Hosted/API AI TTS Landscape for a Remotion Demo

## Assignment

**Goal:** Map current hosted/API text-to-speech services suitable for a public, approximately three-minute, 350–500-word OpenAI Build Week YouTube demo assembled in Remotion on macOS. Prioritize natural narration, reliable pronunciation of technical names, low cost, scriptable generation, and lawful public use.

**Scope:** Internet-only research using current official product documentation, pricing pages, legal terms, privacy/data-use statements, and one independent blind-listening benchmark. The landscape covers 12 candidates, calculates whether disclosed free or trial allowances cover one final narration plus two complete regenerations, and recommends six candidates for later deep dives.

**Exclusions:** No account creation, API calls, audio generation, listening tests, payment-card tests, or local implementation. Voice cloning was inspected only where it affects consent, rights, retention, or plan restrictions; the intended workflow should use licensed stock voices. Product marketing claims about quality are not treated as validated listening results.

## Findings

### Finding 1 — A three-pass campaign is a small workload, but publication rights are the binding constraint

For comparison, this report uses six billable characters per English word, including spaces and punctuation. A 350–500-word script is therefore approximately 2,100–3,000 characters. “Reasonable regeneration” means the final render plus two complete rerenders: 6,300–9,000 characters, or about nine generated audio minutes. This conservative whole-script budget excludes short pronunciation auditions, so a quota with only a few hundred characters of headroom is technically sufficient but operationally tight.

**Evidence:** Character-billed providers expose allowances in characters, while Cartesia exposes approximate minutes. Applying the same 9,000-character ceiling makes the quotas comparable. ElevenLabs separately offers two free regenerations when text and parameters are unchanged, but that policy does not eliminate the need to budget full rerenders when wording or controls change ([ElevenLabs TTS overview](https://elevenlabs.io/docs/overview/capabilities/text-to-speech)).

**Implication:** Every verified free/trial quota except OpenAI’s covers the raw generation workload. Free-tier publication permissions, attribution, account billing setup, and geographic eligibility decide whether that capacity can lawfully and practically be used for the public hackathon video.

### Finding 3 — Speechify is the strongest quality-to-price discovery, with a legal-document freshness gap

Speechify’s free API tier advertises 50,000 characters, 100 minutes, more than 1,000 preset voices, 50+ languages, SSML, speech marks, and JavaScript/Python SDKs. The non-streaming endpoint accepts 2,000 characters and the streaming endpoint 20,000, so the script can be sent in two small deterministic chunks or one stream. PAYG is advertised at $10 per million characters with no commitment. Its SIMBA 3.2 model is one of the strongest current independent blind-preference performers, making it a priority audition candidate.

**Evidence:** The official [API pricing page](https://speechify.com/pricing-api/) and [API limits](https://docs.speechify.ai/docs/get-started/api-limits) state the quotas and endpoint limits. The [AI Voice API supplemental terms](https://speechify.com/terms-ai-voice-api/) treat synthetic output as customer content, permit Public API automation, require commercially reasonable AI-generated disclosure, require explicit written consent for cloned voices, and say “Voices powered by Speechify” must be displayed if Speechify requests it. Artificial Analysis’s current provider-voice arena ranks SIMBA 3.2 among the leading models in blind naturalness voting ([leaderboard](https://artificialanalysis.ai/text-to-speech/leaderboard/provider-voice)).

**Implication:** Allocate a deep dive to test a stock narrative voice against the actual script, verify speech-mark payloads for Remotion, and obtain authoritative confirmation that the free Starter API license permits public competition/YouTube publication. The supplemental terms are dated June 2024 while the model and pricing are 2026-era; plan-specific rights and narration-text retention are not explicit enough to rely on without confirmation.

### Finding 4 — ElevenLabs has the most complete creator workflow, but the free plan is publication-sensitive

ElevenLabs combines expressive long-form models, pronunciation dictionaries or phoneme tags, character-level alignment, many codecs, seed-based best-effort repeatability, and creator-oriented narration guidance. Eleven v3 supplies expressive audio tags and broad language coverage; Multilingual v2 is positioned for stable long-form output; Flash reduces cost and latency. The free allowance can cover the campaign, but the 10,000-character v3/Multilingual quota leaves only 1,000 characters beyond the conservative three-pass budget.

**Evidence:** Official [TTS capabilities](https://elevenlabs.io/docs/overview/capabilities/text-to-speech), [best practices](https://elevenlabs.io/docs/overview/capabilities/text-to-speech/best-practices), and the [timestamps endpoint](https://elevenlabs.io/docs/api-reference/text-to-speech/convert-with-timestamps) document model limits, pronunciation controls, seed behavior, formats, and alignments. Official [publishing guidance](https://help.elevenlabs.io/hc/en-us/articles/13313564601361-Can-I-publish-the-content-I-generate-on-the-platform) says free-plan output is noncommercial and must include `elevenlabs.io` or `11.ai` in the title; paid-plan output receives commercial rights, while beta services do not. Default data handling is not zero-retention; enterprise ZRM is separate, and eligible users can opt out of model-improvement use ([ZRM](https://elevenlabs.io/docs/eleven-api/resources/zero-retention-mode), [data-improvement controls](https://help.elevenlabs.io/hc/en-us/articles/29952728805393-Is-my-data-used-to-improve-ElevenLabs-AI-models)).

**Implication:** ElevenLabs deserves a deep dive for the likely best end-to-end Remotion authoring experience. For a prize competition or any monetized/promotional YouTube use, use a paid plan rather than stretching the free-plan definition of noncommercial; Starter-level commercial licensing is more important than the per-character cost.

### Finding 5 — OpenAI is operationally simple and legally clear, but weaker for precise timeline authoring

GPT-4o mini TTS exposes a single speech endpoint through official JavaScript/Python SDKs, curl, and the OpenAI CLI. Natural-language instructions can control accent, emotional range, intonation, speed, tone, and whispering; 13 built-in voices are English-optimized, with `marin` and `cedar` recommended for quality. MP3 is default, and Opus, AAC, FLAC, WAV, and PCM are available. A dated model snapshot can be pinned for behavior stability.

**Evidence:** The official [TTS guide](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/guides/text-to-speech) documents prompt controls, formats, streaming, CLI examples, voices, and the mandatory disclosure that the voice is AI-generated. The [model page](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/models/gpt-4o-mini-tts) documents a 2,000-token input limit, $0.60/M input text tokens, $12/M output audio tokens, a pinned `gpt-4o-mini-tts-2025-12-15` snapshot, and no API free tier. New API accounts use prepaid billing with a $5 minimum purchase ([billing](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/8264778-what-is-prepaid-billing)). API inputs and outputs are not used for training by default, although abuse-monitoring logs may be retained up to 30 days ([data controls](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/guides/your-data)); OpenAI service terms assign output to the customer ([terms](https://openai.com/policies/service-terms/)).

**Implication:** This is a strong low-friction baseline, especially if an OpenAI billing account already exists. It needs a deep dive because the current docs do not expose SSML, a pronunciation dictionary, word timestamps, or an output-audio-token-to-duration conversion. Pronunciation must be steered with instructions or script respelling and independently auditioned; Remotion timing would need post-generation forced alignment or manual cueing.

### Finding 6 — Google Cloud Chirp 3 HD offers the safest large free allowance and strong pronunciation controls

Chirp 3 HD provides 30 voices, streaming and batch paths, multiple regional endpoints, explicit speaking-rate controls, pauses, and custom pronunciation using IPA or X-SAMPA. SSML support is available in preview for synchronous synthesis, covering `say-as`, `phoneme`, `sub`, `break`, `prosody`, and `voice`. Supported output paths include linear PCM, MP3, Opus, A-law, and μ-law depending on mode. The first one million Chirp 3 HD characters per month are free, then $30 per million.

**Evidence:** [Google pricing](https://cloud.google.com/text-to-speech/pricing) states the monthly free allowance and requires billing to be enabled. The [Chirp 3 HD guide](https://docs.cloud.google.com/text-to-speech/docs/chirp3-hd) documents controls, regions, formats, custom pronunciation, and SSML-preview limits. The generic [SSML guide](https://docs.cloud.google.com/text-to-speech/docs/ssml) supports `<mark>` timepoints for compatible voices, but Chirp 3 HD’s listed preview tags do not include `<mark>`. Google states that Cloud TTS is stateless and does not log submitted text or returned audio ([data logging](https://docs.cloud.google.com/text-to-speech/docs/data-logging)); service terms state that Google will not assert ownership over new IP in generated output or train on customer data without permission ([service terms](https://cloud.google.com/terms/service-terms)).

**Implication:** Allocate a deep dive for pronunciation-heavy technical narration and low-risk data handling. Confirm a suitable English/Australian voice, test SSML preview behavior, and determine whether Chirp returns usable timepoints; otherwise run a separate aligner. Billing activation is required even when usage stays within the free allowance.

### Finding 7 — Murf is unusually well matched to offline video production

Murf offers Australian English stock voices, studio-quality and low-latency models, explicit pronunciation substitutions, pauses, requested audio duration, speed, pitch, variation, and styles. It exports WAV, MP3, FLAC, A-law, μ-law, PCM, and OGG over REST with Python, JavaScript, and curl examples. The API can return word durations, and `encodeAsBase64=true` avoids retained URL-hosted audio; URL output otherwise remains available for 72 hours.

**Evidence:** Official [API pricing](https://murf.ai/pricing?product=api) lists $0.01/K characters for conversational streaming and $0.03/K for studio quality. The [plan-limit page](https://help.murf.ai/murf-api-plans-and-limits) states a 100,000-character free trial. The [overview](https://murf.ai/api/docs/text-to-speech/overview), [customization guide](https://murf.ai/api/docs/capabilities/text-to-speech/speech-customization), [generation endpoint](https://murf.ai/api/docs/api-reference/text-to-speech/generate), and [changelog](https://murf.ai/api/docs/changelog) document the formats, controls, retention behavior, and word durations. Murf’s [terms](https://murf.ai/legal/terms-of-service) permit commercial use of generated output subject to plan and acceptable-use restrictions.

**Implication:** Murf merits a deep dive as the most production-specific candidate for pre-rendered Remotion narration. Validate that `wordDurations` remain stable after pronunciation substitutions and confirm in writing that free-trial output can be published publicly; the general commercial-rights language does not separately discuss trial output.

### Finding 8 — Deepgram combines Australian voices, precise pronunciation, and the largest frictionless credit

Aura-2 includes US, British, Australian, Irish, and Filipino English voice options. Current voice controls support inline IPA pronunciation overrides and speed from 0.7× to 1.5×. REST, official SDK, and CLI workflows can save WAV, MP3, FLAC, or stream audio; encodings also include linear PCM, μ-law, A-law, Opus, and AAC. A $200 credit requires no card, has no stated minimum, and covers far more than this project.

**Evidence:** Official [pricing](https://deepgram.com/pricing) lists $0.030/K characters for Aura-2, $0.015/K for Aura-1, and the no-card $200 credit. The [voice-model list](https://developers.deepgram.com/docs/tts-models), [voice controls](https://developers.deepgram.com/docs/tts-voice-controls), [encoding guide](https://developers.deepgram.com/docs/tts-encoding), and [CLI guide](https://developers.deepgram.com/developer-tools/cli/text-to-speech) document accents, pronunciation, pacing, formats, and export. Deepgram’s business terms grant royalty-free use of output for lawful purposes while prohibiting representing synthetic output as human-generated ([terms](https://static.deepgram.com/business/Business_TOS.pdf)). Non-partnership data is not retained for model improvement; published PAYG pricing is associated with participation in the Model Improvement Partnership, so the privacy/price tradeoff must be checked ([MIP](https://developers.deepgram.com/docs/the-deepgram-model-improvement-partnership-program)). TTS infrastructure is described as US-hosted, which can increase latency from Australia ([latency](https://developers.deepgram.com/docs/text-to-speech-latency)).

**Implication:** Allocate a deep dive for an Australian narrator and difficult product names. The offline export use case tolerates network latency, but official TTS timestamps/alignment were not found. Also reconcile the new IPA-control documentation with an older prompting page that still says custom pronunciation is unavailable.

### Finding 9 — Azure and Polly are capable fallback baselines, not first-wave auditions

Azure Neural TTS has mature SSML for voice, style, role, pitch, rate, volume, emphasis, pauses, pronunciation, and inserted audio. Its SDK emits word-boundary events with audio offsets and character positions; batch synthesis can return word and sentence boundaries. REST supports MP3, Opus, PCM, and WAV with regional endpoints and a ten-minute maximum, comfortably above this script. The F0 tier includes 0.5M neural characters monthly.

Amazon Polly has mature SSML, output ownership, cache/replay rights, and a current Australian generative voice (`Olivia`). Its generative engine is positioned by AWS as the most human-like and supports MP3, OGG, PCM, streaming, and many SSML controls. However, generative voices do not support speech marks; word/sentence/viseme/SSML timing metadata is limited to standard, neural, and long-form engines.

**Evidence:** Azure facts come from its [pricing](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/pricing/details/speech/), [TTS overview](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/ai-services/speech-service/text-to-speech), [REST reference](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/ai-services/speech-service/rest-text-to-speech), [SDK synthesis guide](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-sg/azure/ai-services/speech-service/how-to-speech-synthesis), and [batch synthesis](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/ai-services/speech-service/batch-synthesis). AWS facts come from [Polly pricing](https://aws.amazon.com/polly/pricing/), [generative voices](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/polly/latest/dg/generative-voices.html), [speech marks](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/polly/latest/dg/speechmarks.html), [FAQ](https://aws.amazon.com/polly/faqs/), and its [responsible-AI card](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/ai/responsible-ai/amazon-polly/overview.html), which says standard inputs/outputs are not stored or used to train Polly.

**Implication:** Keep both as skim-only fallbacks. Azure’s current region-specific PAYG price could not be extracted from the official dynamic page, and neither current flagship voice set has enough independent evidence here to justify displacing the six selected deep dives. Polly additionally forces a quality-versus-alignment tradeoff between generative voices and older engines with speech marks.

### Finding 10 — Fish Audio is a compelling seventh candidate, while Resemble lacks decision-grade commercial detail

Fish Audio S2 Pro costs $15 per million UTF-8 bytes with no subscription fee or monthly minimum, supports inline natural-language emotion cues, speed/volume prosody, MP3/WAV/PCM/Opus output, and an SSE endpoint that returns progressive word/segment alignment. Its current selected-voice blind ranking is credible enough to warrant a later audition if a slot opens. However, Fish states that free output is personal-use only and paid plans are required for commercial YouTube use; stock/community voice licensing and retention still need voice-by-voice checking.

Resemble’s Flex plan starts at $0 with pay-as-you-go credits and API access, and Resemble offers its PerTh watermarking technology. The current pricing page, however, does not expose a TTS unit price, initial usable credit, or clear free generation quota. Terms and privacy language do not provide a sufficiently clear stock-voice output license or precise narration-text/audio retention period. Builders Grant terms say output is watermarked by default, but that grant is not the ordinary Flex API plan.

**Evidence:** Fish details are documented in its [pricing](https://docs.fish.audio/developer-guide/models-pricing/pricing-and-rate-limits), [TTS endpoint](https://docs.fish.audio/api-reference/endpoint/openapi-v1/text-to-speech), [timestamp stream](https://docs.fish.audio/api-reference/endpoint/openapi-v1/text-to-speech-stream-with-timestamps), [S2 changelog](https://docs.fish.audio/developer-guide/getting-started/changelog), [commercial-use FAQ](https://fish.audio/), and [terms](https://fish.audio/terms/). Resemble gaps and available controls come from its [pricing](https://www.resemble.ai/pricing), [terms](https://www.resemble.ai/terms-of-service), [privacy policy](https://www.resemble.ai/privacy-policy), and [Builders Grant terms](https://www.resemble.ai/builders-grant-terms-of-service).

**Implication:** Fish is the highest-priority skim candidate and should replace a deep-dive selection if one of the six fails signup, licensing, or listening tests. Resemble should remain out of the audition wave until the vendor supplies current pricing and a controlling stock-output license.

### Finding 11 — Cartesia and PlayAI fail current eligibility checks despite attractive API features

Cartesia would otherwise be a leading option: Sonic 3.5 performs strongly in independent blind preference voting, its API supports streaming, timestamps, speed/emotion control, version headers, and deterministic integration surfaces, and the $5 Pro plan includes commercial rights. The current privacy policy states that the service is designed for users in the United States only and is not intended for users outside the United States. It also permits model-improvement use of input/output by default with an opt-out, while zero-data retention is enterprise-only.

PlayAI exposes streaming formats, seed, speed, emotion, and commercial-use language in its terms. Its current privacy URL begins with the statement, “We have shut down the service,” while documentation and homepage material remain reachable and the pricing URL no longer supplies a verifiable API plan.

**Evidence:** Cartesia’s [pricing](https://www.cartesia.ai/pricing), [SSE API](https://docs.cartesia.ai/api-reference/tts/sse), [terms](https://www.cartesia.ai/legal/terms), [privacy policy](https://www.cartesia.ai/legal/privacy), and [ZDR documentation](https://docs.cartesia.ai/enterprise/zero-data-retention) establish the technical fit, commercial-plan requirement, geographic statement, and data controls. PlayAI’s [API reference](https://docs.play.ht/reference/api-generate-tts-audio-stream), [terms](https://play.ht/terms/), [privacy page](https://play.ht/privacy/), and [pricing URL](https://play.ht/pricing) establish the contradiction.

**Implication:** Reject Cartesia for this Australia-based run unless the vendor directly confirms eligibility under current terms. Reject PlayAI until the company resolves its service-status contradiction and publishes current API billing/quota information.

### Finding 12 — Recommended six deep-dive slots

1. **Speechify SIMBA 3.2** — strongest current independent naturalness signal and very low-cost creator API; deep dive must close free-tier rights and retention gaps.
2. **ElevenLabs Eleven v3 / Multilingual v2** — richest creator controls and alignment, with a clear paid-publication path; audition both expressiveness and stable long-form delivery.
3. **Murf studio-quality TTS** — best explicit offline-video feature fit, including Australian English, pronunciation, duration controls, word timings, and practical zero-audio-retention.
4. **Google Cloud Chirp 3 HD** — largest recurring free allowance among the preferred candidates, strong IPA/X-SAMPA controls, favorable primary-source data handling, and regional infrastructure.
5. **Deepgram Aura-2** — Australian voices, inline IPA, no-card credit, and simple export; verify naturalness and alignment separately.
6. **OpenAI GPT-4o mini TTS** — easiest operational baseline and clear rights/data posture; measure technical-name pronunciation and the cost of external alignment.

**Evidence:** These slots collectively cover the best available independent quality signal, three distinct pronunciation-control approaches (SSML/dictionaries, IPA, natural-language prompting), native alignment and external-alignment workflows, Australian and neutral-English voices, free/no-card/low-cost billing paths, and documented public-use obligations. The classification table and candidate findings above preserve the underlying official evidence.

**Implication:** A later audition should use one fixed 120–180-word excerpt containing every technical name, acronym, emotional transition, and desired pause; generate each candidate with the same target duration and loudness; record exact model/voice/control identifiers; and score blind on naturalness, pronunciation, editability, and rerender stability. Use Fish Audio as the first reserve, Azure as the metadata-heavy reserve, and Polly as the mature-cloud reserve.

## Notes

- **Unsupported quality claims:** Vendor descriptions such as “human-like,” “most reliable,” or “most human-like” were not treated as validated. Artificial Analysis is the only independent subjective evidence used; its live rankings can change, its current filters emphasize US/UK accents, and it does not test this script’s technical names.
- **Operational determinism caveat:** “Deterministic API/CLI/SDK” is interpreted as repeatable scripted generation with fixed model, voice, text, and controls. ElevenLabs calls seed behavior best-effort, OpenAI snapshot pinning stabilizes model behavior rather than guaranteeing identical bytes, and the other shortlisted services do not document bit-identical output.
- **OpenAI cost gap:** Official pages publish text-token and output-audio-token prices but no TTS audio-token-to-duration conversion. The exact cost of nine generated minutes is therefore unsupported; only the $5 minimum prepaid purchase and lack of a free API tier are validated.
- **Speechify legal gap:** The current model/pricing surface is newer than the June 2024 AI Voice API supplemental terms. Free Starter public competition/YouTube rights, narration-text retention, and whether a payment card is required at signup remain unsupported.
- **ElevenLabs publication caveat:** The free quota technically covers the campaign, but free output is noncommercial and title attribution is mandatory. Treat a prize-bearing, sponsored, monetized, or promotional publication as requiring a paid commercial plan.
- **Google alignment gap:** Generic Cloud TTS supports SSML mark timepoints for compatible voices, but Chirp 3 HD’s preview tag list omits `<mark>`. Chirp word/mark timing is unsupported until tested.
- **Murf trial-rights gap:** General terms permit commercial output, but the free-trial documentation does not separately state publication rights or card requirements.
- **Deepgram documentation conflict:** Current May 2026-era voice-control documentation supports inline IPA, while an older prompting page says pronunciation customization is unavailable. Treat the newer model-specific page as the likely current behavior, but validate with an API probe in the deep dive.
- **Watermark/audio-tag gap:** Resemble documents PerTh watermarking and Builders Grant watermark defaults. For the six selected candidates, official sources reviewed specify disclosure or attribution duties where applicable but do not establish whether an inaudible watermark or provenance tag is embedded in ordinary stock-voice output.
- **Geography caveat:** Cartesia is the only candidate rejected for an explicit current user-location statement. Deepgram documents US-hosted TTS infrastructure, which is a latency/privacy consideration rather than an identified Australia eligibility bar. No affirmative global-availability warranty was found for every remaining vendor.
- **Billing/card gap:** Deepgram explicitly states no card is required. OpenAI requires prepaid billing for new API accounts, and Google requires billing enabled. Official current sources inspected did not conclusively establish card requirements for the Speechify, ElevenLabs, Murf, Fish Audio, Azure, or AWS free/trial signup flows.
