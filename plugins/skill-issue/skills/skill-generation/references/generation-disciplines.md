# Generation Disciplines

Apply each installed discipline when its surface is present. The rules below are the minimum inline equivalent when a discipline is unavailable.

- **Skill authoring:** keep the description to one concise purpose sentence plus one concise use-boundary sentence; put output schemas, missing-data behavior, examples, and execution detail in the behavior-changing body; index conditional references; omit auxiliary documentation.
- **Document updates:** locate the semantic owner, make the smallest complete update there, reconcile related meanings, and preserve unrelated meaning.
- **Code implementation:** trace behavior to its concrete owner before editing and verify affected dependants after the owner changes.
- **System ownership:** place each responsibility where the successful system should own it rather than in a convenient wrapper or adapter.
- **Prompt writing:** provide only missing goal, completion criteria, authority, and task-specific context; rely on named authorities instead of reteaching them.

Use the target harness's installed skill-creation or scaffolding authority when one exists. Supply the intake contract and target-specific context rather than duplicating that authority's general procedure.

Reject fixture-specific rules, duplicated owners, speculative flexibility, unnecessary resources, and platform claims unsupported by authoritative evidence.
