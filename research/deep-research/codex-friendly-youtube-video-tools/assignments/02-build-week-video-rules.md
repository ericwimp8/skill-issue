# OpenAI Build Week Demonstration Video Rules

## Assignment

**Goal:** Establish the authoritative OpenAI Build Week requirements and production constraints for the public YouTube demonstration video.

**Scope:** Official rules and the official Devpost event overview, inspected 2026-07-19; video length, audio, content, visibility, project demonstration, Codex/GPT-5.6 explanation, submission materials, intellectual property, assets, and production-relevant eligibility.

**Exclusions:** Video-editor or hosting-platform comparisons; non-official interpretations; general YouTube policy beyond the event's stated requirement.

## Sources

- [OpenAI Build Week Official Rules](https://openai.devpost.com/rules), OpenAI/Devpost, inspected 2026-07-19. Governing source for eligibility, project and submission requirements, intellectual property, and judging.
- [OpenAI Build Week official event overview](https://openai.devpost.com/), OpenAI/Devpost, inspected 2026-07-19. Submission-page guidance that restates the required video, repository, README, and session-ID materials.

## Findings

### Video Format and Visibility

The official rules say the video portion **should be less than three minutes** and state that judges are not required to watch after three minutes. It must be uploaded to YouTube, publicly visible, and linked in the Devpost submission form. [Official Rules — Submission Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The rules use “should be less than three (3) minutes,” then make the judging consequence explicit; they use “must” for public YouTube visibility and providing the link. The event overview independently summarizes this as a “<3-minute public YouTube video.” [Official overview — What to submit](https://openai.devpost.com/)

**Implication:** Plan a complete public YouTube cut below three minutes, placing the essential demonstration and spoken explanation before that boundary. A private, unlisted, locally hosted, or otherwise non-public video does not satisfy the stated rule.

### Required Audio and Narrative Content

The video must include a clear project demo **with audio** covering both what was built and how Codex and GPT-5.6 were used. [Official Rules — Submission Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The governing rules explicitly require a “clear demo with audio” that covers “what you built and how you used Codex and GPT-5.6.” The overview repeats that the audio must cover use of “Codex AND GPT-5.6.” [Official overview — What to submit](https://openai.devpost.com/)

**Implication:** Include intelligible narration or another audible explanation that names and explains both required technologies alongside the visible product demo. Captions can improve accessibility, but the rules do not establish captions as a substitute for audio.

### Demonstration Must Match a Working Project

The project must run consistently on its intended platform and function as the video and text description depict. It must be built with Codex and GPT-5.6, fit one listed track, and have been newly created during the submission period or meaningfully extended with Codex and/or GPT-5.6 after that period began. [Official Rules — Project Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The official rules require successful, consistent installation and operation; they require the product to function as depicted. For an earlier project, they require clear separation of prior and new work plus evidence of Codex/GPT-5.6 use during the period, such as dated session logs or commits. [Official Rules — Project Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Implication:** Capture real, reproducible product behavior rather than a concept-only sequence. For a pre-existing project, align the video story with documented hackathon-period work and retain the required proof.

### Complete Submission Package and Judge Access

Beyond the video, the submission requires a category, feature/functionality description, code-repository URL, and the `/feedback` Codex Session ID for the project thread where most core functionality was built. A public repository needs relevant licensing; a private repository must be shared with `testing@devpost.com` and `build-week-event@openai.com`. [Official Rules — Submission Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The rules require a README explaining collaboration with Codex, key decisions, and how Codex and GPT-5.6 contributed. They also require free, unrestricted testing access through the judging period; the overview adds setup instructions, sample data where needed, and clear run guidance. [Official Rules — Testing](https://openai.devpost.com/rules) [Official overview — What to submit](https://openai.devpost.com/)

**Implication:** Treat the video as one part of an evidence package. Ensure its claims match the repository, README, session ID, description, and testable build; for a developer tool or plugin, include installation, supported platforms, and a no-rebuild test path.

### Music, Visual Assets, Trademarks, and Intellectual Property

The video may not include third-party trademarks, copyrighted music, or other copyrighted material without permission. The full submission must be original, solely owned by the entrant/team/organization, and free of others’ IP, contractual, privacy, publicity, and related rights unless the entrant has the necessary rights or permission. [Official Rules — Submission Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules) [Official Rules — Intellectual Property](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The rules separately allow third-party integrations only when their terms and licensing permit use, and allow open source only with license compliance and entrant-added enhancement. They reserve a non-exclusive judging license for the sponsor and allow promotional use of contributor name, likeness, voice, and image for the hackathon period and three years after. [Official Rules — Project Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules) [Official Rules — Intellectual Property Rights](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Implication:** Use original, properly licensed, or permission-cleared music, footage, images, fonts, screenshots, and brand elements; preserve permission and licence records. Obtain contributor consent before including identifiable voice, likeness, or image. Avoid showing unlicensed third-party products or marks as video decoration.

### Language, Timing, and Post-Submission Control

Submission materials must be in English, or include an English translation of the video demonstration, description, testing instructions, and all other submitted materials. The submission window closes July 21, 2026 at 5:00 pm Pacific Time; after it closes, entrants cannot change or alter the submission, except for narrowly permitted remedial changes approved by the sponsor/Devpost. [Official Rules — Dates and Timing](https://openai.devpost.com/rules) [Official Rules — Language Requirements and Submission Modifications](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The overview displays the same deadline and calls for submission by July 21 at 5:00 PM PT. The rules permit post-deadline changes only to address potential infringement, personally identifiable information, or inappropriate material, while keeping the submission substantively the same. [Official overview — OpenAI Build Week Challenge](https://openai.devpost.com/)

**Implication:** Publish the final English-accessible video and verify its public link before the deadline. Complete all edits beforehand; do not rely on post-deadline replacement for creative or functional improvements.

### Eligibility and Production-Relevant Restrictions

Eligible individual entrants must be at least the age of majority where they live and reside in a territory supported for OpenAI API access, subject to stated geographic, legal, conflict-of-interest, and promoter/judge exclusions. Teams and organizations require an authorized representative who meets the eligibility rules. [Official Rules — Eligibility](https://openai.devpost.com/rules)

**Evidence:** The rules exclude, among others, residents or organizations in unsupported territories, places where participation or prize receipt is prohibited, people connected with promotion or judging, and conflicts of interest. The official overview lists Australia among the displayed eligible territories but directs entrants to the full rules for controlling eligibility details. [Official overview — Who can participate](https://openai.devpost.com/)

**Implication:** Confirm the entrant and representative’s current eligibility before spending on paid production or publishing contributor appearances. If the entry uses collaborators, ensure the entrant owns the submission components and rights needed to submit and promote them.

### Rules Do Not Specify Production Aesthetics

The authoritative material specifies a public YouTube demonstration with audio and the stated content, but does not specify video resolution, orientation, camera presence, editing software, narration voice, captions, thumbnail, or a prescribed visual style. This is an unsupported area rather than a requirement.

**Evidence:** The rules’ video-specific conditions are limited to duration guidance, clear demo with audio, public YouTube upload/link, and rights clearance; the overview gives the same compact summary. [Official Rules — Submission Requirements](https://openai.devpost.com/rules) [Official overview — What to submit](https://openai.devpost.com/)

**Implication:** Select production tools and visual treatment for clarity, speed, and rights safety, while preserving the required demo and audible Codex/GPT-5.6 explanation.

## Notes

- The less-than-three-minute wording is advisory (“should”), but the stated judge-viewing limit makes it a practical ceiling for material that must be evaluated.
- The rules use “GPT 5.6” and “GPT-5.6” in different places; this document preserves the official hyphenated form in recommendations.
- No official source inspected here establishes whether YouTube “unlisted” counts as publicly visible; treat that status as unsupported and publish as public to meet the explicit visibility requirement.
