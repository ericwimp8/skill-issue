# Assisted macOS Screen Recorders

## Assignment

**Goal:** Determine whether Screen Studio, Tella, CleanShot X, or FocuSee is worth trialing or paying for instead of free native capture or OBS for one polished, generic, public 1080p YouTube demonstration under three minutes for the OpenAI Build Week submission.

**Scope:** Current first-party product, pricing, legal, privacy, and support material inspected on 2026-07-19. Assessment covers macOS availability, trial/refund and watermark/export entitlement, 1080p/4K, automated visual polish, editing/audio/caption capability, cloud and rights considerations, Codex/computer-use fit, user recording actions, output-quality evidence, and expected hands-on time.

**Exclusions:** Purchasing, installing, signing in, recording product footage, verifying an individual Mac's hardware/performance, and researching general editors or native/OBS capture in depth. A vendor feature claim is not a hands-on quality benchmark.

## Findings

### Decision: trial Tella first; avoid paying unless a short hands-on comparison proves material value

For this single deadline-bound cut, **trial Tella first** if the project footage is appropriate for a hosted/cloud workflow. Its official seven-day, no-card Pro trial, local Mac recorder, clean downloadable video, 4K capability, automatic zoom, edit/caption/audio controls, and export provide the broadest source-backed end-to-end option without purchase. Capture only the app demonstration there, then use the free baseline/editor selected elsewhere for the required opening/closing treatment if Tella's layouts and title treatment are insufficient.

**Evidence:** Tella says every account starts a seven-day full-Pro trial without a card; the trial share page carries a small badge but the video itself is not watermarked. It advertises recording/export up to 4K, automatic zoom, separate screen/camera tracks, system audio, Studio Voice, and a full editor. Its plans page lists 4K, timeline/transcript editing, captions, background music, layouts, crop, blur/highlight, AI audio enhancement, exports, and a native macOS recorder. [Tella product/FAQ](https://www.tella.com/) [Tella plans](https://www.tella.com/pricing) [Tella help](https://www.tella.tv/help/introduction/plans)

**Implication:** Do one contained capture-to-export trial before the final recording. Pay only if the trial's result demonstrably saves more than the time of native/OBS capture plus free finishing. Screen Studio has the strongest local automatic-polish proposition, but its currently advertised US$29 monthly / US$99-per-month-equivalent annual subscription and non-refundable terms make it a poor first choice for one video. FocuSee's free test exports are watermarked, while CleanShot's polished-capture features do not replace a real video editor.

### Screen Studio: best local automatic polish, but paid and refund-risky for one cut

Screen Studio is the narrowest screen-demo specialist: it automatically follows actions with zoom, smooths/enlarges/replaces cursors, adds framing/background/shadow, supports cuts, speed changes, camera, microphone/system audio, local transcription/subtitles, audio normalization/noise removal, and export up to 4K/60 FPS. It is well suited to a beautiful app-only demonstration with little keyframe work, but its own published editor feature set does not establish title-card or transition authoring; use another editor for the required brief animated intro/outro if needed.

**Evidence:** The product page says macOS Ventura 13.1+ is recommended; automatic zoom, cursor smoothing, backgrounds, trim/cut/speed, webcam/mic/system audio, automatic local transcript/subtitles, audio cleanup, and 4K/60 FPS video/GIF export are stated directly. It lists US$29 monthly or US$9/month billed yearly. The current terms say subscriptions are prepaid and non-refundable; the public pricing page does not state a trial or an unwatermarked trial-export entitlement. [Screen Studio product/pricing](https://screen.studio/) [Screen Studio terms](https://screen.studio/legal/terms-of-service)

**Implication:** It is worth paying only if the team explicitly values its distinctive auto-zoom/cursor aesthetic enough to accept at least US$29 and the no-refund term. A price-only experiment is not justified ahead of Tella's trial, native capture plus a free editor, or OBS. Export entitlement/watermark for an unpurchased evaluation is **unsupported** by the reviewed first-party material; do not assume a usable trial exists.

### Screen Studio privacy, rights, and Codex boundary

Screen Studio's normal recording/editing route is local: its policy says screen recordings remain on-device unless the user explicitly creates a shareable link. It documents local subtitle generation and exposes a newly prepared external URL schema in its changelog, but the reviewed sources do not document a stable public command for starting and stopping a capture or fully automating a project edit. Its legal material is a software/service subscription agreement, not a separate stock-media/commercial-output licence.

**Evidence:** The privacy policy says recordings are processed locally and are never uploaded unless a shareable link is created; it also warns that blur is a creative tool, not legal redaction, and retains the original in the project file. The product page says subtitle generation occurs on the machine. The May 2026 changelog says a custom URL schema was being prepared for Raycast integration, without an API contract. [Screen Studio privacy](https://screen.studio/legal/privacy-and-cookie-policy) [Screen Studio product](https://screen.studio/) [Screen Studio changelog](https://screen.studio/changelog)

**Implication:** Codex can generate the narration, shot list, timing, on-screen copy, SRT/subtitle draft, and any source visual assets; computer-use can assist ordinary UI editing after installation. The owner must grant macOS Screen Recording, microphone, camera, and file-save permissions and deliberately perform/oversee the product capture and final export. Publish original footage only after clearing app content, logos, music, and any assets separately; a positive Screen Studio commercial-output grant was not found.

### Tella: strongest no-purchase all-in-one trial, with cloud and AI processing trade-offs

Tella combines native macOS capture with a hosted editor and video platform. It records screen, camera, microphone, and system audio; preserves screen and camera as separate tracks; applies automatic zoom around clicks; and offers transcript/timeline editing, silence/filler removal, captions, background music, layouts/backgrounds, blur/highlight, logo overlay, links, embeds, analytics, and downloads. That breadth can reduce hands-on time for a single narrated demo, though it still benefits from a deliberate script and a brief final review of captions/auto-zoom.

**Evidence:** Tella says the Mac app requires macOS Ventura 13+, clips may run up to six hours, and paid plans have unlimited video storage/bandwidth. Its product and plan pages state 4K recording/export, automatic zoom, separate screen/camera tracks, system/microphone audio, edit/caption/audio tools, and 4K download; 60 FPS is a Premium feature. Pro is currently US$13/month annually and Premium US$19/month annually. [Tella FAQ](https://www.tella.tv/help/introduction/faq) [Tella product](https://www.tella.com/) [Tella plans](https://www.tella.com/pricing)

**Implication:** Trial Tella when its hosted processing is acceptable and the user wants the fastest screen-demo route with spoken cleanup/captions. It is especially compelling for the hackathon's required clear demo with audio, but it is not the local-first choice. The automatic output is a starting point: review focus points, caption accuracy, and final file before YouTube upload.

### Tella pricing, watermark, public rights, and privacy need intentional handling

Tella's trial is unusually practical for this task: no video watermark and no card, only a small trial badge on its hosted share page. A paid first purchase has a 14-day full-refund guarantee. The user keeps ownership of user content, but Tella receives a broad licence needed to host/back up/share it; the service stores personal data in the United States and may process user content through Gemini when AI features are used. Its terms allow business use but place rights/consent responsibility on the user.

**Evidence:** The product FAQ makes the trial/badge/no-video-watermark distinction; the legal policy gives first paid purchases a 14-day no-questions refund. The terms say users retain User Content, grant Tella a worldwide perpetual sublicensable service-performance licence, and may use the service for their own business or personal use; the user warrants the rights/consents required. The privacy policy lists video/voice/face data, US storage, and says Tella does not otherwise access video recordings; the terms say AI features may process User Content through Gemini. [Tella product](https://www.tella.com/) [Tella refund policy](https://www.tella.com/legal/refund-policy) [Tella terms](https://www.tella.com/legal/terms-of-service) [Tella privacy](https://www.tella.com/legal/privacy-policy)

**Implication:** A public YouTube file exported from Tella has a source-backed clean-video trial path, but do not upload credentials, private customer data, or unreleased material just for speed. The terms support ordinary business use of the user's own material, not unrestricted rights in third-party music, stock, UI content, or branding. Codex can create the script, shot plan, title/caption text, and visual assets; browser/computer-use can edit the hosted project, while the user must sign in, authorize capture devices, initiate recording, and approve publication.

### CleanShot X: fast local capture companion, not a polished single-tool editor

CleanShot X is a native macOS capture utility with local MP4 H.264/GIF recording, selectable window/area/fullscreen capture, resolution/FPS/quality controls, microphone/computer audio, cursor/click/keystroke/camera overlays, notification suppression, desktop cleanup, trim, resolution/volume edits, and optional cloud links. It makes a fast, clean screen-only capture or supporting clips, but the documented video editor only trims and adjusts technical/audio settings: it does not establish automated zoom, captions, titles, transitions, or a multi-clip finishing timeline.

**Evidence:** CleanShot lists US$29 one-time for the app plus a year of updates and 1 GB cloud storage; later updates are optional at US$19/year. It requires macOS 10.15+, has no direct trial but a 30-day money-back guarantee, and is available in Setapp with a seven-day trial. Its feature page documents the recording controls and constrained video editor. Cloud is optional and dedicated to uploads/share links; its fair-use policy explicitly includes sharing work with clients and social-media posting. [CleanShot pricing/FAQ](https://cleanshot.com/pricing) [CleanShot features](https://cleanshot.com/features) [CleanShot fair use](https://cleanshot.com/fair-use)

**Implication:** Do not buy it solely for this final video. Trial only through an existing Setapp subscription or use the refund route if the team already wants a durable screenshot/capture utility. It is a reasonable low-setup local capture fallback, but the visual polish and assembly still require a free editor; therefore its total hands-on time for this deliverable can exceed Tella or Screen Studio despite fast recording.

### CleanShot X: local-first behaviour and limited automation are positives

CleanShot Cloud is not required for capture or other app features. It offers a documented URL-scheme API for capture actions, making it the clearest programmatic capture launcher of the four, but the documented record actions do not remove the need for the owner to choose the intended screen/window, approve macOS permissions, verify sensitive content, and stop/export a recording.

**Evidence:** CleanShot's FAQ says Cloud is only used for screenshot uploading and is not required for the app. Its URL-scheme documentation provides supported capture commands; its feature page describes optional recording upload/share links, password protection, and self-destruct links. The EULA licences use of the application but does not state a special commercial licence for generated video. [CleanShot FAQ](https://cleanshot.com/pricing) [CleanShot URL-scheme API](https://cleanshot.com/docs-api) [CleanShot features](https://cleanshot.com/features) [CleanShot EULA](https://cleanshot.com/eula)

**Implication:** Codex can generate a repeatable capture plan and, after user configuration, a URL-scheme invocation; use computer-use for post-capture trimming only. Preserve the user-only security actions and final inspection. Treat its product terms as no explicit prohibition on publishing original output, while separately clearing all visible content/audio and not relying on an unverified commercial-output grant.

### FocuSee: feature-rich automated alternative, but its free export is watermarked

FocuSee is closest to Screen Studio in features: full/window/area capture, auto/manual zoom, cursor styling/click effects, captions, text-based editing, screen highlight/blur/mosaic, canvas/background styling, 3D motion, noise reduction, AI voice enhancement, silence/filler removal, MP4/GIF output, and resolutions through 4K. It is source-backed as capable of a polished YouTube screen-demo master after activation, but its free route should be used only to evaluate the flow because the vendor's billing FAQ states the one free exported video carries a watermark.

**Evidence:** FocuSee documents MP4/GIF and 480p through 4K output. Its Mac App Store listing documents auto/manual zoom, caption adjustment, background/canvas/cursor/highlight controls, and a post-recording editor. The billing FAQ says free users can record/edit and export one video with a watermark, while activated plans remove all watermarks and allow unlimited 4K/60 FPS exports. Its public pricing page separately says the free trial can export one high-quality 4K video; the FAQ supplies the critical watermark qualification. [FocuSee recording guide](https://focusee.imobie.com/guide/record-video.htm) [FocuSee App Store listing](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/focusee-screen-recorder/id6472626992) [FocuSee billing FAQ](https://focusee.imobie.com/faq/presales-and-billing.htm) [FocuSee pricing](https://focusee.imobie.com/pricing.htm)

**Implication:** It is worth a brief free comparison recording only if Tella's cloud route is unsuitable and Screen Studio's local aesthetic is desired. It is not a free final-export solution. Purchase only after confirming its actual rendered 1080p clip, caption quality, and export entitlement on this Mac; no independent hands-on quality evidence was gathered.

### FocuSee price, requirements, permissions, and rights require more caution than the trial pitch

The current single-device Standard price is US$19.99 monthly or US$49.99 yearly; annual plans have a 60-day money-back guarantee, monthly plans seven days. A US$199.99 one-time Advanced plan covers five devices and FocuSee 2.x, while AI functions consume time-limited credits. FocuSee supports macOS 10.15+, recommends 12.3+, needs 8 GB RAM and Apple M1+ or an Intel Mac, and asks for screen/camera/microphone permissions and sometimes an audio driver. The reviewed sources do not establish a complete privacy/data-processing statement or a positive commercial-output licence.

**Evidence:** Pricing/refund/credit conditions are in the official pricing page and iMobie policy. The installation guide gives the Mac system requirements; the permissions guide says screen/camera/microphone access and driver installation are required, although macOS 13+ can record audio without an extra driver. The App Store listing characterises it as Mac-only, while the vendor's own current guide also documents Windows; this assignment uses the vendor's Mac requirements for the requested macOS path. [FocuSee pricing](https://focusee.imobie.com/pricing.htm) [iMobie refund policy](https://www.imobie.com/support/refund-policy.htm) [FocuSee requirements](https://focusee.imobie.com/guide/install-or-uninstall.htm) [FocuSee permissions](https://focusee.imobie.com/guide/permissions-and-settings.htm)

**Implication:** Avoid enabling credit-backed AI features unless their result is needed and acceptable; consumed credits reduce a refund. Codex can create all editorial inputs and computer-use can assist ordinary GUI editing, but the user must install the app/driver, approve macOS permissions, authenticate/purchase, record, and inspect the final. Keep sensitive material out until a current privacy policy and the exact AI/cloud behaviour are reviewed. For public/commercial use, clear captured content and third-party assets separately; the exact FocuSee output licence is **unsupported** in the reviewed sources.

### Comparative quality, speed, and ownership assessment

For output polish from one uninterrupted product demo, Screen Studio has the strongest local, source-described automation; Tella has the strongest trialable end-to-end narration/caption/editing package; FocuSee is a credible paid/trial comparison with more AI effects; CleanShot is fast capture rather than automatic video polish. Those rankings are feature-based inferences, not measured rendering, codec, accessibility, or visual-quality benchmarks.

**Evidence:** Screen Studio explicitly describes automatic cursor/action zoom and smoothing, local transcript/subtitles, audio cleanup, and 4K/60 output. Tella describes automatic click zoom, transcript/timeline editing, captions, audio cleanup, 4K recording/export, and its trial. FocuSee documents auto zoom, canvas/cursor/caption/AI edits, and paid no-watermark 4K/60; CleanShot documents capture overlays and trim/technical edits rather than a visual timeline. [Screen Studio](https://screen.studio/) [Tella](https://www.tella.com/) [FocuSee FAQ](https://focusee.imobie.com/faq/presales-and-billing.htm) [CleanShot features](https://cleanshot.com/features)

**Implication:** Use free native/OBS capture plus free finishing as the baseline: it remains the lowest-cost, local-control path and avoids an unfamiliar paid workflow. Spend one short trial only on Tella first; use FocuSee only as a local-style comparison; select Screen Studio only by explicit purchase decision; and use CleanShot only when it is already available or the reusable capture utility itself is valuable. Any selected tool must still be paired with a source-backed public YouTube upload check and an owner-led final recording/export pass.
