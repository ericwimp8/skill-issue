# Codex-Friendly YouTube Video Tools Research Map

## Run Goal

Identify a free, minimal, fast, LLM-friendly tool or small toolchain for producing a polished, generic, public 1080p YouTube demonstration under three minutes for an OpenAI Build Week submission. The result must combine screen footage, basic intro/outro animation, occasional transitions, audio, and final stitching, while making substantial practical use of Codex.

## Framing

The final synthesis must give the best-supported direction, conditional alternatives, rejected or lower-fit interpretations, evidence, and unresolved blockers. It must include a ranked shortlist, decision matrix, recommended workflow, division of Codex and user work, setup and time estimates, pricing/free-tier restrictions, licensing/privacy risks, and an immediately actionable recommendation.

- Source scope: local plus internet
- Active researcher concurrency: 8

## Broad Research Domains

1. Local and official Codex capability boundaries
2. Competition video requirements and submission constraints
3. Scriptable and code-defined video ecosystems

Discovery researchers classify candidates as deep-dive, skim-only, or reject. Deep-dive assignments are selected from their evidence while preserving eight remaining researcher slots.

## Expected Evidence

- Current official feature, pricing, free-tier, watermark, export, platform, privacy, and licensing pages
- Official repositories, releases, package documentation, and license files for open-source candidates
- Current official OpenAI/Codex documentation and verified local capability inventory
- Authoritative competition rules
- Credible practitioner reports only for usability caveats, clearly labeled as forum-derived
- Small non-destructive local probes where they materially validate installed capabilities

## Fan-Out Decision Rules

- Prefer direct scriptability/headless operation, then reliable browser/desktop automation.
- Select deep dives for the strongest distinct production paths, not near-duplicate products.
- Preserve a free/open-source path and a minimal-GUI path even if a paid trial is easier.
- Reject candidates with unavoidable watermarks, inadequate 1080p export, unsuitable macOS support, unclear current availability, or disproportionate learning/setup cost.
- Classify unverified claims as unsupported or blocked rather than carrying them into the synthesis as facts.
