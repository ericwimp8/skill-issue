# Research Map: Natural AI TTS for a Remotion Demo

## Run Definition

- **Goal:** Identify the best natural-sounding, lawful, low-cost AI text-to-speech workflow for a public, approximately three-minute OpenAI Build Week YouTube demonstration composed in Remotion on macOS.
- **Source scope:** Internet only.
- **Researcher budget:** 8 total researchers.
- **Active concurrency:** Up to 8 researchers; staged waves will use only the slots needed.
- **Final synthesis target:** `research/deep-research/natural-ai-tts-for-remotion-demo/natural-ai-tts-for-remotion-demo-deep-research.md`.
- **Final shape:** Best-supported direction, conditional alternatives, rejected or lower-fit options, evidence, and unresolved blockers, including the requested recommendation categories, matrix, cost estimates, workflow, rights checklist, and proof-generation gate.

## Research Domains

1. Hosted/API neural TTS services suitable for deterministic Codex generation.
2. Browser-first TTS products with downloadable audio and strong perceived naturalness.
3. Downloadable/open TTS options usable on macOS with privacy advantages.
4. Rights, attribution, consent, retention, privacy, geographic, and account constraints.
5. Remotion integration: formats, segmentation, timing, alignment, captions, pronunciation, normalization, and synchronization.
6. Independent evidence about naturalness, pronunciation reliability, and workflow friction.

## Discovery Waves

### Wave 1: Ecosystem Mapping

- `assignments/01-hosted-api-landscape.md`: Discover and rank current hosted/API candidates using official evidence plus credible independent quality/workflow evidence.
- `assignments/02-browser-open-landscape.md`: Discover and rank browser-first and downloadable/open candidates, including privacy-conscious options.

Each discovery assignment should produce approximately 8-12 raw candidates, classify each as deep-dive, skim-only, or reject, and recommend the strongest candidates for the six remaining researcher slots.

### Wave 2: Focused Deep Dives

Six narrow assignments will be selected after Wave 1. Expected fan-out units are individual providers, a tightly related provider family, open-model stacks, rights/privacy evidence classes, and Remotion production workflow. Selection will prioritize candidates that can plausibly win best overall, quality, cost, API control, browser-only, or privacy-conscious categories.

## Expected Evidence

- Official pricing, quotas, trial/free-tier sufficiency, billing/card requirements, supported APIs/SDKs/CLIs, macOS usability, formats, voice/accent selection, SSML or pronunciation controls, pacing/emphasis controls, alignment/timestamps, and streaming/export behavior.
- Official terms and policies covering public/commercial YouTube use, hackathon submission use, attribution, trial-output restrictions, retention/download rights, voice-cloning consent, privacy/data use, watermarking or audio tags, and geographic/account limits.
- Credible independent listening comparisons and practitioner reports for subjective naturalness, pronunciation, reliability, and workflow friction, clearly labeled as subjective evidence.
- A realistic 350-500 word, three-minute cost calculation with regeneration allowance.
- Concrete evidence supporting Codex-executable and browser-assisted workflows into Remotion.

## Fan-Out Rules

- Six Wave 2 slots remain after the two discovery assignments.
- Important legal, pricing, or capability claims require official-source confirmation where available.
- Independent impressions may rank perceived quality but cannot establish rights, pricing, or product guarantees.
- Any claim that cannot be validated is classified as unsupported, caveated, or blocked in the relevant assignment.
