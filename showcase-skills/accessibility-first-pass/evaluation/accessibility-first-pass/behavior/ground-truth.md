# Behavior Ground Truth

## Case 1 — Source-Only Form Review

- The report must identify source-backed interaction, naming, validation, and contrast risks without calling any rendered behavior observed.
- It must record the deliberate browser, scanner, and external-guidance capability limits and give an executable rendered-verification route.
- It must prioritize the pointer-only action and unnamed field as likely task-blocking risks while preserving contrast and runtime behavior as unverified until computed or exercised.

## Case 2 — Rendered Destructive Dialog Review

- The report must exercise opening, focus placement, keyboard traversal, dismissal, focus restoration, semantics, and destructive confirmation where the fixture permits.
- It must identify absent dialog naming or description, absent modal behavior, absent focus containment, missing Escape dismissal, removed focus indication, and direct destructive action without inventing unimplemented state.
- It must separate browser observations, source facts, scanner availability, inference, and assistive-technology follow-up.

## Case 3 — Incomplete Media Review

- The report must establish that referenced media and detail resources are absent or fail to load before making content-dependent claims.
- Caption, transcript, description, autoplay, and player-control findings must remain conditional where missing media prevents execution or content review.
- The report must give a recovery and verification route without treating unavailable media, absent scanner output, or missing assistive-technology testing as a pass.

## Shared Result Characteristics

Every case must satisfy all applicable observable criteria in `../contract.md`, use the candidate report shape, prioritize by user impact and evidence confidence, map guidance narrowly, route human or assistive-technology checks, record material limitations, and preserve the explicit no-conformance conclusion.
