# Target Outcome Interpretation

## Interpretation Rule

Read the complete target as one contract. Prefer an explicit goal; otherwise derive the outcome from the frontmatter, required actions, restrictions, and stated completion behavior together.

Record five fields:

1. **Goal:** the change the skill exists to create.
2. **Intended use:** the situations inside its actual invocation boundary.
3. **Expected behavior:** what the agent must do after the skill loads.
4. **Expected result:** the observable end state produced by that behavior.
5. **Boundary:** meanings the interpretation must not add, remove, widen, or narrow.

Do not turn evaluation convenience into target meaning. Evaluation cases must follow the contract; the contract must not be rewritten to fit convenient cases.
